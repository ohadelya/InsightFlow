# Product Principles

## Non-Negotiable Principles

1. Never invent facts.
Every conclusion must be grounded in document evidence or clearly marked uncertainty.

2. Always bind conclusions to evidence.
Decision statements must be explainable by observable signals, not stylistic inference.

3. Show only what helps a decision.
Output should optimize decision quality, not maximize text volume.

4. Missing information is valuable information.
Critical omissions are decision signals and must be surfaced explicitly.

5. The dashboard must reduce reading time.
If users still need to fully reread documents for first-pass decisions, the product failed.

6. Expert reasoning before visualization.
Display structure follows decision logic, not the other way around.

7. Performance builds trust.
Slow responses degrade confidence and reduce practical adoption.

8. Every recommendation must be actionable.
Recommendations must contain a concrete next step, not abstract advice.

9. Preserve uncertainty honestly.
Confidence and ambiguity must be visible where evidence is weak.

10. Standardize decision quality.
The same document should produce consistent decision framing across users and runs.

11. Optimize for operational reality.
Outputs must fit real workflows: screening, negotiation, bid qualification, and planning.

12. Product value over model novelty.
The product is judged by decision outcomes and time-to-decision, not by generated prose quality alone.
